EgoMimic-Mobile | Scaling Mobile Manipulation through Egocentric Human Data

Anonymous Authors1,

Abstract

Recent works have attempted to tackle mobile manipulation via teleoperation, but collecting mobile teleoperated demonstrations is prohibitively slow and expensive at scale. We present EgoMimic-Mobile, a novel end-to-end framework for collecting and training mobile manipulation policies with only static robot data combined with mobile human data. Our approach consists of three key components: 1) a data collection pipeline that maps human motion to a differential-drive robot base; 2) a training framework that seamlessly co-trains on both human and robot data; and 3) an adaptive inference regime that intelligently switches between manipulation and navigation modes. Through direct transfer of human navigation knowledge with kinematics re-targeting, we achieve zero-shot transfer of navigation capabilities across embodiments without expensive mobile robot data collection. In our experiments across three challenging long-horizon tasks, EgoMimic-Mobile demonstrates superior performance compared to baselines trained on teleoperated mobile robot data, achieving higher task performance in our scoring system with significantly less data. We find that EgoMimic-Mobile is able to generalize to new spatial configurations and scenes, and we observe positive performance scaling as we increase the hours of human data, opening new avenues for scalable robotic learning in real-world environments.
